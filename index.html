Want some special spice to make your site stand out? Want to interact with the user and store their feedback? Katapult can do that. If you're looking for someone who is flexible to take on as little or as much as you need and to do quality, diligent work in a timely manner - look no futher than Katapult Coding and Web Dev.

The journey began the summer of 1998 when Kathryn Bolster, the founder and heart and soul of Katapult, started on a graduate degree at Rochester Institute of Technololgy in software development. She had just graduated magna cum laude from Nazareth College with a degree in Political Science and Pre-Law, however, had decided that pursuing a career in law did not fit with the lifestyle she had envisioned for herself. Therefore she decided to pursue a career in the exciting realm of software development.

Shortly after she started her summer course work for the graduate program she received a call from Keane, Inc. (now part of NTT Data), a company that had her resume from a job fair she had attended. They were recruiting people who excelled in math to take an aptitude test to see if they qualified to be trained in assisting with Y2K code remediation. While this wasn't exactly the cutting edge technology she was expecting to learn at RIT, it was paid for and it was a job doing programming so she took advantage of the opportunity. After acing the apptitude test she was shuttled to Boston for a 3 month intensive training program that taught her the basics of programming. Shortly after Y2K Keane invested in Kathryn for a second time and sent her to Boston for 2 months of client/server training. Here she had her first introduction to object oriented programming, database design and implementation, and concepts of how clients and servers interact. She really enjoyed this training and was thrilled to incorporate a visual aspect to her coding.

As the Y2K boom waned and Keane's opportunties dried up Kathryn went to work for a 2-man shop that specialized in custom web development. Being the third person in a company that at the time was designing and developing data-driven websites, stand alone shopping carts and working with large area businesses to develop client-server solutions for their manufacturing lines allowed her to grow her skills tremendously. PHP, Javascript, SQL Server, VB, and C++ were some of the skills she honed while working there. On top of on-the-job learning she also took a graduate level course in C++ at Syracuse University. It was a right of passage the owner put on her. He knew the course was tough and wanted to put his new employee through her paces. While the course was challenging, Kathryn earned an A and learned a great deal.

The beauty of working in a tiny company is the ability to touch every aspect of every project. An employee is forced to be a jack of all trades and the learning opportunity is immense. This was the perfectly appropriate job for Kathryn to learn the skills she would need to further her career. When work was scarce and the company shifted focus Kathryn did some contract client/server programming for about a year before accepting a full time position as a programmer/anaylst for a finacial services company. At this job Kathryn did a great deal of database work with both SQL Server and Oracle. She also developed multiple applications using the Microsoft .NET framework. Kathryn also completed the Syracuse University MS .NET Application Developer Program, an intensive study of 3 focused modules that immersed you into the various nuances of the development environment, SQL Server,C# and VB.NET. From the financial services company Kathryn got a job close to home at a medical device manufacturer and worked there for a decade. During her time there she started her family and eventually went to part time. Now that her children are older and she has more time she is eager to get back into developing applications and beautiful websites. No stranger to learning on her own she has taken various classes to get up to speed on the latest technologies and tools such as HTML5/CSS, the bootstrap framework, PHP, jQuery, Javascript, mySQL, NoSQL, GIT, etc. She also has the advantage of all her experience and understanding of how things work and fit together. She has coded the things in the past that current tools now do for you and has a comprehensive understanding of what is going on beneath the hood of an application. If you're looking for someone who is flexible to take on as little or as much as you need and to do quality, diligent work in a timely manner - look no futher than Katapult Coding and Web Dev.
